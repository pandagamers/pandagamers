Pandamonium Apprenticeship Process

Pandamonium is a member-driven community and as such we need feedback from all our members. We cannot emphasize enough that we greatly appreciate and always take into consideration all feedback on potential new members.

Can't see the Open Applications section or Apprentice Review channels?
Scroll to the top of the channel listing and click on "Channels & Roles." Click on "Browse Channels" on desktop or "All Channels" on mobile.
  1. For Apprentice Reviews, scroll down to the Information section and click on the checkbox next to "apprentice_review."
  2. For Applications, scroll down to the "Open Applications" category (third category down). Click on "Follow Category."

Applications
Once a community application has been submitted, it is accessible by those ranked as Member and above in the Open Applications category. All community members are encouraged and welcome to submit their comments within those underlying channels. A simple Yes or No reaction will also suffice. Once an applicant has been accepted, future issues or concerns should be brought directly to the attention of Leadership.

Criteria For Review
1. Did they take the application process seriously (i.e., provide enough information so that we were able to get a basic sense of them as a person)?

2. Do they appear to be a well-rounded player? If they go on in great detail about their min-maxing experience and singular focus, giving no real detail about who they are as a person, they likely won't fit in here. Our community is a diverse group of people with a variety of playstyles and our base is built on social interaction. If someone is not interested in more than hardcore content, this is likely not the right place for them.

3. Did you get a good or bad vibe? We realize this is hard to quantify. Even if a specific issue doesn't jump out at you, getting a bad vibe from an app is usually reason enough to deny.

4. Does it look like they would run afoul of our diversity statement? "Our members represent differences in race, age, gender, politics, religion, and sexual orientation. Those inclined towards bigoted, racist, homophobic, transphobic, xenophobic, or misogynistic behavior need not apply. You wouldn't like it here, and we wouldn't like to have you." This is a no brainer.

Keep In Mind
1. For friends or family of current members, please encourage them to go through the process as if we were strangers (because most of us are). It's unlikely they would be denied entry due to a crappy application, but it's not the greatest of first impressions that should be given to our members.

2. The application comments section should not be used as a forum to convince other members that their point of view is wrong. Comedic banter is fine. Replying to say you agree is fine. Asking people to justify their position or calling them out in some negative way is not productive. People should not worry that they are going to be attacked (even if done respectfully) for their opinions. Simply share your thoughts on an app and let other folks do the same. Trust that the process (and the Moderators who are approving or denying) will work.

3. Do not use past applications as justification for approving or denying a new one (i.e., "We approved Michael's app and that one was just as crappy as this one, so shouldn't we approve this one too?"). All apps should be reviewed on their own.

4. Above all else, be respectful of your fellow community members. If you take significant issue with the comments someone is making and feel like your response will be harsher than is appropriate, please speak with a member of Leadership.

Apprentice Reviews
After a minimum of 30 days, Apprentices are put up for an initial review with the community. We encourage members to be vocal about their experiences with Apprentices, whether the commentary is good or bad. Moderators use this information to approve or deny an Apprentice for membership, so please be vocal in passing on these thoughts. If you've had a positive interaction with an Apprentice, that's enough for a "Yes" vote. Any yellow flags warrant a "Needs more time" vote. A "No" vote is for red flags. Please share any related information for a "No" or "Needs more time" vote in the post or with a moderator. Before an Apprentice is promoted to Member, any comments on them will be deleted.

An Apprenticeship can be extended for another two weeks due to inactivity or not enough "Yes" votes at which time they'll be up for a final review.

If there are issues with an Apprentice, don't wait for the review to be posted. Contact a member of the leadership team or #open-a-ticket with the Moderators so the issue can be addressed.

The membership process was always intended to be collaborative so that everyone is comfortable with who is coming in the door. Thus far we think it's worked out very well. Keep it up!
